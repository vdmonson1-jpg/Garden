# Pepper

> *Capsicum annuum* (and related species) | Solanaceae

## Overview

Peppers are warm-season crops closely related to tomatoes, offering a wide spectrum from sweet bells to fiery habaneros. They are slower to mature than tomatoes but reward patience with heavy yields and excellent flavor. Both sweet and hot types are grown similarly.

## Growing Conditions

| Factor           | Details                        |
|------------------|--------------------------------|
| Sun              | Full sun (6–8 hours)           |
| Water            | 1–2 inches per week            |
| Soil pH          | 6.0–6.8                        |
| Soil Type        | Well-draining, fertile loam    |
| Spacing          | 12–18 inches apart             |
| Hardiness Zone   | Annual in most zones; perennial in zones 9–11 |
| Days to Maturity | 70–90 days                     |

## Planting

Start seeds indoors 8–10 weeks before last frost — peppers need more lead time than tomatoes. Soil temperature for germination should be 80–85°F. Transplant after last frost when nights stay above 55°F. Peppers do not like cold soil.

## Care

### Watering
Consistent moisture is key. Allow top inch of soil to dry slightly between waterings. Overwatering causes root rot; underwatering causes blossom drop. Drip irrigation is ideal.

### Feeding
Moderate feeder. Apply balanced fertilizer at planting. Once fruiting begins, reduce nitrogen to avoid lush foliage at the expense of fruit set.

### Pruning & Training
Light pruning improves airflow. Stake or cage larger varieties. Pinching early flowers can encourage larger, more vigorous plants (optional).

### Pest & Disease
Aphids, spider mites, pepper weevils, bacterial spot, phytophthora root rot. Inspect regularly, rotate crops, ensure good drainage.

| Good Companions         | Bad Companions     |

## Harvest

Sweet peppers can be harvested green or left to fully ripen to red, yellow, or orange — flavor deepens with ripening. Cut with scissors or pruners; twisting can damage the plant. Hot peppers: harvest based on heat preference; fully ripe = maximum heat.

## Varieties to Try

- **Shishito** — Mild, thin-walled; great for blistering in a pan
- **Jimmy Nardello** — Sweet Italian frying pepper; exceptional flavor
- **Serrano** — Hot; earthy, clean heat; productive plant
