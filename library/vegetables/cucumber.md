# Cucumber

> *Cucumis sativus* | Cucurbitaceae

## Overview

Cucumbers are fast-growing warm-season vines that produce abundantly in summer heat. They come in slicing, pickling, and specialty types. With a trellis, they take up minimal ground space and are easier to harvest. A consistent water supply is essential for mild-flavored, crisp fruit.

## Growing Conditions

| Factor           | Details                        |
|------------------|--------------------------------|
| Sun              | Full sun (6–8 hours)           |
| Water            | 1–2 inches per week            |
| Soil pH          | 6.0–7.0                        |
| Soil Type        | Fertile, well-draining         |
| Spacing          | 12 inches (trellised), 36 inches (bush) |
| Hardiness Zone   | Annual; grows best in zones 4–11 |
| Days to Maturity | 50–70 days                     |

## Planting

Direct sow after last frost when soil is warm (65°F+). Can start indoors 2–3 weeks before transplant date but does not like root disturbance — use biodegradable pots. Plant in hills or rows.

## Care

### Watering
Deep, consistent watering is critical. Inconsistent moisture leads to bitter fruit. Water at the base; wet foliage encourages powdery mildew.

### Feeding
Moderate feeder. Amend soil with compost before planting. Side-dress with balanced fertilizer when vines begin to run.

### Pruning & Training
Train vines onto trellis for vertical growing. Pinching lateral shoots once vines reach desired height can concentrate energy. Remove yellowing leaves.

### Pest & Disease
Cucumber beetles (spreads bacterial wilt), powdery mildew, downy mildew, squash vine borers. Use row covers until flowering. Plant resistant varieties.

| Good Companions         | Bad Companions     |

## Harvest

Harvest frequently to keep plants producing. Slicers: 6–8 inches. Picklers: 2–4 inches. Over-ripe cucumbers turn bitter and signal the plant to stop producing. Check daily at peak season.

## Varieties to Try

- **Marketmore 76** — Classic slicer; disease resistant
- **Spacemaster** — Compact bush type; good for containers
- **Lemon** — Yellow, round; mild flavor; conversation starter
