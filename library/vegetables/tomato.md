# Tomato

> *Solanum lycopersicum* | Solanaceae

## Overview

The tomato is one of the world's most popular garden vegetables, prized for its versatility and rich flavor. It thrives in warm conditions and produces abundantly through the summer season. Available in hundreds of varieties ranging from tiny cherry types to large beefsteaks.

## Growing Conditions

| Factor           | Details                        |
|------------------|--------------------------------|
| Sun              | Full sun (6–8 hours minimum)   |
| Water            | 1–2 inches per week            |
| Soil pH          | 6.0–6.8                        |
| Soil Type        | Rich, well-draining loam       |
| Spacing          | 18–36 inches apart             |
| Hardiness Zone   | Annual in most zones; perennial in zones 10–11 |
| Days to Maturity | 60–85 days (variety dependent) |

## Planting

Start seeds indoors 6–8 weeks before last frost. Transplant after all frost risk has passed and soil has warmed to at least 60°F. Plant deep — bury up to 2/3 of the stem to encourage strong root development. Direct sowing outdoors is not recommended in most climates.

## Care

### Watering
Water deeply and consistently — 1–2 inches per week. Water at the base to avoid wetting foliage. Inconsistent watering causes blossom end rot and cracking. Mulch heavily to retain moisture.

### Feeding
Heavy feeder. Apply balanced fertilizer at planting, then switch to lower-nitrogen, higher-phosphorus/potassium formula once flowering begins. Calcium is critical — prevent deficiency with lime or gypsum amendments.

### Pruning & Training
Indeterminate varieties benefit from staking or caging. Remove suckers (shoots growing between stem and branch) for larger fruit and better airflow. Pinch terminal growth in late season to redirect energy to ripening fruit.

### Pest & Disease
Common threats: aphids, hornworms, whiteflies, early blight, late blight, fusarium wilt. Use row covers early, inspect regularly, remove affected leaves. Rotate crops annually to reduce soil-borne disease.

## Harvest

Harvest when fully colored and slightly soft to the touch. Twist or cut from the vine. Store at room temperature — refrigeration dulls flavor. Green tomatoes will ripen indoors off the vine.

## Varieties to Try

- **Sun Gold** — Prolific orange cherry; exceptionally sweet
- **Cherokee Purple** — Heirloom beefsteak; rich, complex flavor
- **Roma** — Paste type; meaty, low moisture; great for sauce
