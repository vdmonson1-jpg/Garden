# Basil

> *Ocimum basilicum* | Lamiaceae

## Overview

Basil is an aromatic annual herb indispensable in Italian and Southeast Asian cuisines. It thrives in heat, loves full sun, and grows quickly from seed. It is one of the best companion plants for tomatoes, reportedly improving flavor and repelling pests. Pinching flowers regularly keeps plants bushy and productive.

## Growing Conditions

| Factor           | Details                        |
|------------------|--------------------------------|
| Sun              | Full sun (6–8 hours)           |

| Spacing          | 12–18 inches apart             |
| Hardiness Zone   | Annual; grown in all zones     |
| Days to Maturity | 24–28 days to first harvest    |

## Planting

Start seeds indoors 4–6 weeks before last frost, or direct sow after frost danger has passed. Soil temperature should be at least 70°F for germination. Transplant carefully — basil is sensitive to cold and root disturbance.

## Care

### Watering
Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged. Water at the base; wet leaves in hot sun can scorch. Reduce watering slightly once established.

### Feeding
Light to moderate feeder. A balanced fertilizer or compost at planting is usually sufficient. Avoid over-fertilizing with nitrogen, which dilutes flavor.

### Pruning & Training
Pinch flower buds as soon as they appear to prolong leafy growth. Harvest from the top down, cutting above a leaf node to encourage branching. Never remove more than 1/3 of the plant at once.

### Pest & Disease
Aphids, Japanese beetles, slugs, fusarium wilt, downy mildew. Ensure good airflow. Remove infected leaves promptly.

## Harvest

Begin harvesting when plant has at least 6 sets of leaves. Cut stems just above a leaf node. Best flavor before flowering. Harvest in the morning after dew dries.

## Varieties to Try

- **Genovese** — Classic large-leaf Italian basil; best for pesto
- **Thai** — Anise/licorice flavor; holds up to heat better in cooking
- **Purple Ruffles** — Ornamental and edible; striking color
