# Cilantro

> *Coriandrum sativum* | Apiaceae

## Overview

Cilantro (also called coriander) is a fast-growing cool-season herb prized for its bright, citrusy leaves in Latin and Asian cuisines. It bolts quickly in heat, making succession planting essential for a continuous harvest. Both the leaves and seeds (coriander) are culinary staples.

## Growing Conditions

| Factor           | Details                        |
|------------------|--------------------------------|
| Sun              | Full sun to partial shade      |
| Water            | Moderate; consistent moisture  |
| Soil pH          | 6.2–6.8                        |
| Soil Type        | Well-draining, loamy           |
| Spacing          | 6–8 inches apart               |
| Hardiness Zone   | Annual; cool-season crop       |
| Days to Maturity | 21–28 days to first harvest    |

## Planting

Direct sow is preferred — cilantro has a taproot and resents transplanting. Sow in early spring or fall (cool weather is key). Stagger plantings every 2–3 weeks for continuous harvest. Lightly press seeds into soil; needs light to germinate.

## Care

### Watering
Keep evenly moist. Drought stress accelerates bolting. Mulch to retain moisture and keep roots cool.

### Feeding
Light feeder. Amend soil with compost before sowing. Avoid high-nitrogen fertilizers which push leafy growth but hasten bolting.

### Pruning & Training
Harvest outer leaves regularly to delay bolting. Once flowers appear, flavor diminishes — let it go to seed for coriander, or pull and replant.

### Pest & Disease
Aphids, spider mites, leaf spot. Generally low-maintenance. Good airflow helps prevent fungal issues.

## Companion Planting

| Good Companions              | Bad Companions |
|------------------------------|----------------|
| Tomato, Spinach, Anise       | Fennel         |
| Beans, Peas (attracts beneficials) |         |

## Harvest

Snip leaves from the outside of the plant. Best harvested young before flower stalks form. Allow some plants to bolt and collect dried seeds for coriander spice.

## Varieties to Try

- **Slow Bolt** — As the name implies; longer harvest window in warm weather
- **Santo** — Standard variety; heavy leaf producer
