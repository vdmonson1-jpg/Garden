## Soil & Amendment Notes

- **Do not heavily amend wildflower beds.** Wildflowers bloom better in moderately lean, well-drained soil.
- Excess organic matter = moisture retention = fungal pressure in Louisiana humidity.
- If soil seems depleted: ½" Black Kow max, mixed lightly into surface.
- **Priority is drainage and structure, not fertility.**
- If drainage is poor: add pine bark fines or expanded shale (10–20% by volume, top 6–8").
- Do not add blood meal (too much nitrogen for wildflowers) or heavy compost layers.

## Notes

- Lower portion reserved for kids' digging / fairy garden area.

- That narrow strip is better suited for: herbs, lettuce, carrots, green onions, marigolds, zinnias, or compact flowers.
- Wildflowers prefer lean soil — dense foliage + few blooms usually means over-fertilization.
