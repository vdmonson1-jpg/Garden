## Sun Map

Beds run east–west. Full sun exposure. South-facing edges get strongest direct sun. Tomatoes tolerate intense Louisiana sun well. Bell peppers placed at the far end get full, unobstructed light.

## Current Plants

| Position              | Plant                              | Spacing                     |
|-----------------------|------------------------------------|-----------------------------|
| Center line           | Cherry Tomatoes (indeterminate) x2 | 24–30" apart, prune to 1–2 main stems |
| Between tomatoes      | Basil x2                           | 1 plant between each tomato |
| Far end of bed        | Bell Peppers x2                    | 18–24" apart                |
| Near peppers (edges)  | Green Onions                       | 2–3" apart, 3–6" from pepper stems |
| Near peppers (edges)  | Garlic                             | 4–6" apart                  |
| Outer edge (walkway)  | French Marigolds                   | 8–10" apart                 |
| Existing              | Snapdragons (fall holdovers)       | Leave in place until heat decline |
| Existing              | Pansies/Violas (fall holdovers)    | Leave in place; do not move |

## Companion Planting

- **Basil** between tomatoes: deters hornworms and whiteflies; improves airflow if pruned.
- **French Marigolds** along outer edge: nematode suppression, beetle/aphid deterrent.
- **Green onions + garlic** near peppers: mild aphid/beetle deterrent. Keep 3–6" from pepper stems. Do not place garlic where zucchini leaves would cover it.

## Maintenance

- Prune tomatoes to 1–2 main stems to prevent shading.
- Remove lower tomato leaves once fruit sets (airflow in humidity).
- Clip or string-train tomatoes vertically.
- Keep bases of tomato stems open — no dense clustering.
- Avoid overhead watering.
- Mulch heavily to manage heat and humidity.
- Snapdragons and pansies will naturally decline in summer heat — plant around them, do not relocate.

## Notes

- Heavy feeders — amend with Black Kow composted manure (½–1" worked into top 4–6").
- Drainage issue history: standing water, hydrophobic topsoil. Corrective plan: fork-aerate 8–12" deep, incorporate pine bark fines (10–15% by volume, top 6–8").
- 18" deep, open to native ground (crumbly/sandy subsoil — good drainage below).
- Walkways on all sides.
