# Box 3 — Cucumber & Zucchini Bed (North Side of Trellis)

## Specs

| Attribute       | Details              |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Dimensions      | 24" wide, ~6' along trellis x 18" deep |
| Material        | Cedar                |
| Orientation     | E-W                  |
| Sun Exposure    | Full (north side of arch — slightly less intense than south) |
| Trellis         | Yes — arched wire trellis shared with Box 4 |
| Irrigation      | Hand water           |

## Sun Map

Beds run east–west. This bed is on the north side of the arch trellis. Gets strong sun but slightly softer afternoon exposure than the south side. Cucumbers appreciate this — they tolerate intense sun but benefit from slight relief in midsummer Louisiana humidity.

## Current Plants

| Position                | Plant                    | Spacing                       |
|-------------------------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Trellis line            | Cucumbers x3             | 18–24" apart, 6–8" off-center from trellis |
| Near trellis (offset)   | Kale x1–2                | 10–12" from cucumber stems; transitional spring crop |
| Near trellis (offset)   | Arugula (clusters)       | 6–8" spacing; transitional spring crop |
| Far end past trellis    | Zucchini x1              | Last 2–3' of bed; allow to spill outward |
| Outer edge              | Nasturtiums              | Spill outward over bed edge   |
| Near end of bed         | Dill                     | Toward one end                |
| Outer edge              | French Marigolds         | Along walkway side            |

## Companion Planting

- **Nasturtiums**: trap crop for aphids and cucumber beetles. Let spill over edge. Keep from climbing into cucumber stems.
- **Dill**: attracts parasitic wasps (beneficial for hornworm control). Place toward end of bed.
- **French Marigolds**: soil pest suppression along outer edge.
- **Kale + arugula**: short-term spring crops. Will bolt in sustained heat (85°F+). Replace with heat-tolerant green (e.g., Malabar spinach) mid-summer.

## Maintenance

- Train cucumbers onto trellis immediately — they latch with tendrils.
- Trim older cucumber leaves near the base once fruiting starts (airflow).
- Limit zucchini to last 2–3' of bed. Remove older lower leaves as it matures.
- Do not plant garlic on the edge where zucchini will spill — leaves will smother it.
- Check zucchini stems regularly for squash vine borer eggs. Consider wrapping lower stems with foil or netting early season.
- Avoid more than 3 cucumber plants — overcrowding increases powdery mildew risk.
- Stay consistent with watering; both crops are heavy feeders.
- Mulch heavily.

## Notes

- Zucchini spreads 3–4' wide. One plant only. Expect it to spill beyond the bed.
- Kale and arugula are both brassicas — they attract the same pests (cabbage loopers, aphids, flea beetles). If one gets infested, the other will too. Do not cluster all brassicas together.
- Drainage issue history: same as other beds. Fork-aerate and add pine bark fines before planting.
- 18" deep, open to native ground (crumbly/sandy).
- Walkways on all sides.
