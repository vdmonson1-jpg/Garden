## Soil Structure Notes

- **Issue observed**: Standing water on surface; water pools then slowly drains, but soil stays dry below wet layer.
- **Diagnosis**: Hydrophobic / compacted upper layer from excess fine organic matter. Surface sealing.
- **Native ground below** (at 18" depth): Crumbly, slightly sandy — good drainage. Problem is confined to raised bed layer.
- **Corrective plan**:
  1. Deep fork aeration (8–12" deep, every 8–10" across bed). Rock fork to fracture soil. Do not flip layers.
  2. Incorporate pine bark fines (10–15% by volume) into top 6–8".
  3. Rehydrate slowly: light water, wait 10–15 min, repeat.
  4. Keep amendments light going forward (½" max Black Kow).
  5. Avoid heavy compost layers, blood meal, or bone meal unless deficiency observed.
