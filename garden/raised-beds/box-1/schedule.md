|------------------|--------------|--------------------------------------|
| Rosemary         | Established  | Begin shaping every few weeks        |
| Sage             | Established  | Pinch tips; cut longer stems by ⅓    |
| Tricolor Sage    | Established  | Pinch tips; cut longer stems by ⅓    |

| Parsley          | Established  | Harvest outer stems at base weekly   |

| Oregano          | To add       | Plant between sage and rosemary      |
| Lemon Thyme      | To add       | Plant along bed edges                |
| Chives           | To add       | Plant between spreading herbs        |
| Greek Basil      | To add       | Plant near parsley                   |

## Ongoing Maintenance Cycle

| Frequency     | Task                                            |
|---------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| Every 7–10 days | Harvest tips from each herb (cut above leaf node) |
| Monthly       | Shape woody herbs — shorten stems 25–30%        |
| Monthly       | Deadhead marigolds                              |
| Every 4–6 weeks | Light feeding (avoid high nitrogen)           |
| As needed     | Deep water when soil dries slightly; avoid daily watering |
| Mid-season    | Cut back any leggy/sparse herbs by ⅓ for regrowth |

## Succession Planting Notes

- Parsley may bolt in sustained summer heat. Succession plant or replace.
- Greek basil is more heat-tolerant than standard basil — should last longer into summer.
- If any herb declines in peak summer, assess whether to replace or let it recover in fall.
