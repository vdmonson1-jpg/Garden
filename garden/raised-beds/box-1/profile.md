## Sun Map

Beds run east–west. South-facing side receives strongest direct sun. Full sun exposure throughout the day. Herbs need full sun for dense growth — partial shade produces tall, thin stems.

## Recommended Additions (for fullness)

Three-layer strategy: tall anchors, mid-size fillers, low spreaders.

| Layer          | Plant              | Why                                    | Placement                    |
|----------------|--------------------|----------------------------------------|------------------------------|
| Mid-size filler | Oregano           | Dense spreading mounds; fills gaps between woody herbs | Between sage and rosemary |
| Low spreader   | Lemon Thyme        | Compact carpet (4–6"); covers bare soil along edges | Bed edges                |
| Vertical accent | Chives            | Thick clumps + purple flowers; height variation | Between spreading herbs  |
| Mid-size filler | Greek Basil (small-leaf) | Stays compact and bushy; tolerates heat | Near parsley (similar moisture) |
| Low spreader   | Creeping Marjoram  | Gentle spread; dense foliage; softens edges | Between larger plants    |
| Optional       | Cuban Oregano      | Thick succulent leaves; grows large fast; excellent in Louisiana heat | Open area with room to spread |

## Companion Notes

- Rosemary and thyme prefer drier soil than parsley — group by water needs.
- French marigolds along the outer edge attract beneficials and provide mild nematode suppression.
- Keep marigolds compact (French varieties, not tall African types). Deadhead regularly to prevent mildew.
- Greek basil near parsley (both tolerate more moisture than Mediterranean herbs).

## Maintenance — Getting the Bed Full

### Harvesting (Most Important for Fullness)

- **Every 7–10 days**, trim a few stems from each plant.
- Cut just above where two leaves meet the stem (leaf node). Two new stems grow from each cut.
- Never remove more than ⅓ of a plant at once.
- Rotate harvest areas — cut from different stems around each plant for symmetrical growth.

### Herb-Specific Pruning

| Herb           | Technique                                  |
|----------------|---------------------------------------------|
| Parsley        | Cut outer stems at the base; leave center growth intact |
| Sage / Tricolor Sage | Pinch new growth tips regularly; cut longer stems back by ⅓ in spring/early summer |
| Rosemary       | Trim soft green growth only; avoid cutting into thick woody stems; shape every few weeks |
| Thyme          | Shorten longer stems by 25–30% monthly; cut only green flexible growth |

### Monthly Shaping

- Once a month, shorten longer stems on woody herbs by ~25–30%.
- Cut only green flexible growth, not thick woody stems.

### Mid-Season Reset

- If any herb becomes tall and sparse, cut back by about ⅓.
- Regrows thicker within 2–3 weeks.
- Only do this during warm growing months.

### Watering

- Most herbs prefer soil drying slightly between watering.
- Deep watering instead of frequent light watering.
- Parsley tolerates more moisture; thyme, sage, and rosemary prefer drier conditions.
- Overwatering causes leggy growth in Louisiana humidity.

### Feeding

- Use slightly lean soil — too much fertilizer produces tall, weak growth.
- Light feeding every 4–6 weeks.
- Avoid high-nitrogen fertilizers.
